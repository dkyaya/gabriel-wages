- **Police/fire safety sources**: yes (Somerville police, Wayland fire, Franklin police + fire, Georgetown police — 5 of 10 rows).
- **Arbitration/factfinding/impasse-heavy source**: yes — Somerville police (full JLMC interest-arbitration opinion) and Wayland fire (JLMC stipulated award), both `source_type=arbitration_award`.
- **Public works/DPW**: yes — Franklin public works (1 row; the only clean, matched-city DPW example available within the 10-row cap).
- **Clerical/admin**: yes — Boston clerical/admin.
- **Library**: yes — Franklin library.
- **Custodial/facilities or other mixed municipal**: yes — Franklin other (custodians) and Georgetown other (custodians), 2 rows.
- **Dispatch/public-safety-adjacent or nurse_health**: **not included this run.** The only Massachusetts corpus row with this content, `ma_wayland_other_2021`, is `text_quality=ocr_messy` with a 48-page, 270°-rotated scan that `pdftotext` extracts as ~0 usable characters (confirmed directly this session). A prior session's `pre_report_must_have_evidence_review_2026-07-06.md` reports successfully hand-OCR'ing specific passages from it (dispatcher pay grades, a Community Health Nurse pay grade and stipend), but did not save that OCR text to a durable file, and re-doing a bounded OCR pass was judged not worth the scope for a *curated, capped* sample when the task itself lists this category as "if useful" (optional), not a hard requirement like police/fire/DPW/clerical/library. **This is a known, flagged gap**, not a silent omission — recommended as a specific follow-up (a small, one-row OCR-focused codify addendum) rather than folding it into this run.
- **At least two matched Massachusetts cities/towns**: yes — Franklin (5 occupation classes: police, fire, public_works, library, other) and Georgetown (2: police, other) are both fully matched within this 10-row sample.

## Caveats

- Franklin dominates this sample (5 of 10 rows) because it is the only Massachusetts city in the corpus with 5 clean, extractable occupation classes in one place — this is a deliberate choice to maximize genuine cross-occupation, same-city, same-cycle comparison (the design's central discipline), not an oversight.
- Somerville and Boston are institutionally important but unmatched *within this specific 10-row sample* — both already have `data/contracts.csv` metadata confirming this (Somerville has no non-safety `data/contracts.csv` row at all; Boston's only other collected row, police, is unusable text). This does not mean they are unmatched in `data/city_coverage.csv` more broadly — only that this particular codify sample does not include a second occupation class for them.
- No dispatch/nurse_health coverage this run (see gap above).
