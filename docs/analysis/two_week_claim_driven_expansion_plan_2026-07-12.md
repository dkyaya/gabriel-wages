# Two-Week Claim-Driven Expansion Plan - 2026-07-12

## 1. Objective

Greatly expand the causal corpus across the country so future claim-centered reports can test and refine the leading hypotheses with evidence and reasoning. This is not a report-writing phase. The next two weeks should produce source scans, target lists, ingestion candidates, controlled ingestion batches, codify-ready waves, and claim-status updates.

## 2. Expansion Principles

- Matched city triads are highest value: police + fire + at least one genuine non-safety/general municipal unit in overlapping cycles.
- Non-safety sources are bottleneck sources and should be prioritized early in every state scan.
- Interest arbitration, factfinding, formal impasse, and wage-study sources are high value for testing conversion-channel and peer/comparator hypotheses.
- Repeated cycles are useful but secondary to expanding matched city/state coverage in this two-week wave.
- No FOIA/PRR. Use only public, findable sources or already-authorized intake routes.
- Keep causal corpus separate from context/discourse sources. Do not force discourse into `contracts.csv`.
- Recognition-clause-first classification remains mandatory for broad non-safety units.
- Evidence should be organized by hypotheses/claims, with the 19 GABRIEL/codify attributes used as measurement tools.

## 3. Recommended Two-Week Structure

### Week 1

1. Run source-availability scans for tier-1 states in this order: Pennsylvania, New Jersey, Illinois, New York.
2. Build state/city target lists with candidate URLs, source portals, and non-safety availability notes.
3. Prioritize the cleanest matched triads; do not ingest a state where only safety sources are findable unless they serve a specific arbitration/factfinding/comparator hypothesis.
4. Ingest the cleanest matched triads only after bounded criteria are met: source is public, provenance is clear, safety and non-safety units are locatable, and recognition clauses support classification.
5. Update source-needs and claim-target matrices after each state scan.

### Week 2

1. Continue ingestion for the best states from Week 1; demote states with poor non-safety availability.
2. Run GABRIEL/codify batches in controlled waves only after ingestion batches are validated and scoped. Do not run codify during the source-scan prompt.
3. Refresh the claim register and claim-evidence matrix after each codify wave.
4. Prepare only a short internal claim-status memo if needed, not a full report.
5. Reserve final claim-centered report drafting for closer to the deadline, after the national evidence layer is materially larger.

## 4. Suggested Target Scale

These targets are ambitious and should yield to source quality:

- Screen at least 4-6 new states.
- Ingest 2-4 new states if public matched sources support it.
- Add 8-15 new cities/towns if source availability supports it.
- Add 25-50 new causal sources as a plausible ambitious target.
- Add at least 6-10 new matched triads if possible.
- Prioritize quality, provenance, and match discipline over raw count.

## 5. Decision Gates

- Stop expanding a state if non-safety sources are unavailable after a reasonable public-source scan.
- Promote a state if it offers clean matched triads and institutional contrast.
- Defer a state if source burden is high and claim-testing value is low.
- Do not let safety-only evidence dominate the next corpus wave unless it serves a specific claim such as peer/comparator evidence in awards or formal impasse backstops.
- Treat safety-adjacent units as exploratory unless the recognition clause and institutional setting justify a specific comparison role.

## 6. Deliverables

- National source target CSV.
- State scan memos and CSVs for tier-1 states.
- Ingestion batches with provenance and coverage updates.
- Codify batches after ingestion, in controlled waves.
- Updated claim register and claim-evidence matrix after codify waves.
- Final claim-centered report only near the deadline, when the expanded evidence base can support it.
