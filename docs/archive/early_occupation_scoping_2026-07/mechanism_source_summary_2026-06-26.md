# Mechanism-Source Recon Summary

**Date:** 2026-06-26

## Purpose

This note consolidates the bounded public-source recon for Newton, Somerville, and Boston. The goal was to see whether those cities expose non-safety wage-reasoning materials that help interpret the current GABRIEL v9 pattern without expanding the causal corpus, rerunning GABRIEL, or using public-records requests (PRRs).

## What was searched

The pass reviewed only the already identified public routes: school-system labor pages, union bargaining pages, School Committee materials routes, and a few targeted public presentations or downloads. It did not rerun a broad search. Across the three cities, the reviewed materials were mostly bargaining updates, proposals, mediation materials, settlement summaries, School Committee presentations, Collective Bargaining Agreement (CBA) pages, Memorandum of Agreement (MOA) leads, and meeting-material indexes.

## Main finding

Boston produced the clearest public non-safety peer-wage comparison lead. The Boston Public Schools and Boston Teachers Union negotiations page includes an explicit surrounding-district salary comparison, making it the strongest public non-safety comparability signal found in this recon.

Newton and Somerville were still useful, but mainly as wage-rationale or bargaining-process evidence. Newton exposed a rich proposal, mediation, and settlement trail from the 2023-2024 teacher negotiations. Somerville exposed public educator contract and ratification materials. But neither city produced a clean non-safety award, factfinding report, or Joint Labor-Management Committee (JLMC)-style reasoning document comparable to the safety-side Somerville police awards already in the corpus.

## What this means for H1

H1 remains plausible but underidentified. Public evidence now shows at least one non-safety peer-wage comparison route, but mostly as a mechanism proxy rather than as a causal document. Source-type confounding therefore remains central: explicit comparability reasoning is still easiest to observe in award-style or bargaining-explanation documents, while many ordinary CBAs and MOAs mainly preserve outcomes.

## City-by-city summary

### Newton

Newton produced the richest public bargaining-process trail. The Newton Teachers Association and Newton Public Schools routes exposed proposals, mediation outlines, package comparisons, a February 2024 MOA lead, and a strike-settlement summary. These materials are useful mechanism proxies because they show wage rationale and bargaining movement. But the inspected Newton materials did not produce a clean peer-district wage-comparison exhibit or a non-safety award/factfinding equivalent.

### Somerville

Somerville produced useful educator-side public materials, especially a ratification release and clean 2025-2028 educator CBAs. Those documents help show public wage framing and settlement language, but they do not solve the main mechanism gap. The key missing item is still a non-safety reasoning document comparable to the Somerville police arbitration awards.

### Boston

Boston remains the strongest lead. The Boston Public Schools and Boston Teachers Union negotiations page contains a surrounding-district salary comparison and direct public wage-rationale language. The April 2025 School Committee presentation adds further public explanation around wage structure, low-wage worker increases, and implementation. These are still mechanism proxies, not final causal-corpus documents, but they are the clearest public non-safety comparability materials found so far.
