## 3. Arlington dispatchers

**Evidence reviewed:** direct `pdftotext` re-read of `ma_arlington_public_works_2015`'s full CBA text (1,863 lines, native text, no OCR needed). Cross-checked against the `binding_arbitration_statute` field for all three Arlington `public_works` cycles.

**Findings:**
- **Explicit in the CBA — a dedicated article, not an incidental mention.** Article XXI is titled "Community Safety Dispatchers" and contains 13 numbered provisions covering staffing complement, schedule, shift-bidding, sick-call-in backfill, mandatory overtime rotation, vacation-coverage restrictions, and an EMD (Emergency Medical Dispatch) certification stipend.
- **Same bargaining unit as non-safety AFSCME workers.** Article II's recognition clause lists Community Safety Dispatchers as category 6 of 7, alongside Labor Service (DPW), Custodial service, Civil engineers, Clerical personnel, and Administrative personnel — explicitly excluding uniformed police/fire personnel and (separately, under item 5's exclusions) "Medical Personnel." Dispatchers are civilian AFSCME members, not sworn officers, confirmed directly by the recognition clause's own structure.
- **A real, explicit minimum-coverage and staffing-complement rule.** "A full complement of Community Safety Dispatchers will consist of nine (9) persons and a Lead Dispatcher... Coverage must be maintained by a full complement of two (2) dispatchers at all times excluding emergency situations."
- **Wage/classification information is only partially visible.** There is no per-classification base-wage table anywhere in this CBA — only a long, unit-wide history of across-the-board percentage increases (e.g., "Effective July 1, 2015 – 2%... Effective July 1, 2017 – 2%") that applies identically to dispatchers, DPW, custodians, and clerical staff alike. The actual dollar base rates live in an external "Classification and Pay Plan" (referenced as adopted July 1, 2001) that is not itself part of this CBA and is not in this project's corpus. A shift-premium rate for dispatchers is visible directly (30 cents/hour for the midnight-to-8am shift; time-and-a-half for holidays worked), and a $300/year EMD stipend is visible, but no base annual salary figure for the dispatcher classification specifically.
- **Public-safety-adjacent features are strong and multiple.** Vacation approval for dispatchers requires "prior approval of the Chief of Fire or Police" — a direct, textual example of civilian dispatchers reporting through public-safety command authority despite AFSCME bargaining-unit membership. The EMD stipend requires "a certified E911 telecommunicator" credential plus CPR and EMD certification. Easter Sunday is "considered a holiday for Public Safety Dispatchers" — the document itself uses "Public Safety Dispatchers" and "Community Safety Dispatchers" interchangeably.
- **Institutional status confirmed, not merely assumed.** All three Arlington `public_works` rows' `binding_arbitration_statute` field reads "MA G.L. c. 150E" — the ordinary Chapter 150E route, not JLMC/MA G.L. c. 1078. This directly closes the previously-open DP-D03 question (whether dispatchers are Chapter 150E-covered by extension) for Arlington specifically: they are, confirmed by the metadata field itself, not merely assumed.

**Short snippets:**
> "A full complement of Community Safety Dispatchers will consist of nine (9) persons and a Lead Dispatcher... Coverage must be maintained by a full complement of two (2) dispatchers at all times excluding emergency situations."

> "No more than one (1) dispatcher to be on vacation at one time without prior approval of the Chief of Fire or Police."

> "In order to act as a certified EMD dispatcher one must be a certified E911 telecommunicator; obtain and maintain CPR certification; and obtain and maintain EMD certification."

**Classification: `confirmed_public_safety_adjacent`.**

**Recommended treatment:** Keep as a public-safety-adjacent comparison case. Dispatchers are richly, directly evidenced in this project's own corpus (a dedicated CBA article, real minimum-staffing language, real credential/stipend language, and civilian-status-under-public-safety-supervision language) but institutionally confirmed as Chapter 150E, not JLMC. The wage-level gap (no per-classification base table in this CBA) is a genuine, documented limitation — not a reason to defer the group, but a reason to flag any future wage-level claim about dispatchers specifically as unsupported by this document alone.

## 4. Custodial/facilities

**Evidence reviewed:** direct `pdftotext` re-reads of `ma_georgetown_other_2020` (1,085 lines, native text) and `ma_franklin_other_2022` (953 lines, native text), plus the custodial-relevant sections of `ma_arlington_public_works_2015` already reviewed in §3.

**Findings by row/city:**

- **Georgetown (`ma_georgetown_other_2020`).** A dedicated School Department CBA "REPRESENTING CUSTODIANS," covering "all custodians, matrons and maintenance employees of the Georgetown School Department." Contains a full **"CUSTODIAL AND MAINTENANCE SALARY SCHEDULE"** (Appendix A) with 4-step wage tables for four distinct classifications: Custodians, "PB & MHS Head Custodians," Licensed Maintenance, and Unlicensed Maintenance — the last two showing a clear, quotable credential-to-pay differential (Licensed Maintenance Step 1 = $55,034.22 vs. Unlicensed Maintenance Step 1 = $49,461.32, roughly an 11% premium for licensure), directly analogous to DPW's already-confirmed CDL/hoisting-license-to-pay linkage. A direct outsourcing clause exists: "The district reserves the right to outsource custodial/maintenance in the event of any emergency or during a case of unforeseen circumstances... No outsourcing will be done to replace existing or routine custodial/maintenance duties."
- **Franklin (`ma_franklin_other_2022`).** A dedicated municipal (not explicitly school-only) CBA covering "all full-time custodial and maintenance/custodial employees." Contains a 5-step **hourly-rate salary schedule** (Appendix A, separate tables for FY2023/2024/2025) with four classifications: Junior Building Custodian ($21.30-$25.73/hour across 5 steps), Senior Building Custodian A (Single Elementary Schools), Senior Building Custodian B (Complex Schools/High School), and Maintenance Custodian. A direct subcontracting-restraint clause exists: "No subcontracting shall be used for normal cleaning of Public School buildings in order to avoid overtime, unless an emergency situation exists or overtime is refused." A snow/ice-response overtime premium exists ("If overtime is related to snow or ice removal, custodians will be paid two times (double) their regular hourly [rate]"), directly parallel to DPW's storm-response overtime mechanism. A detail-pay clause exists ("If a Custodian works a detail assignment paid by an outside vendor, they shall be paid at a rate of one and one half... times the highest Senior Custodian rate"), a genuine parallel to police officers' Massachusetts paid-detail income channel.
- **Arlington (`ma_arlington_public_works_2015`, bundled).** Custodial service is recognition category 2 of 7 in the same broad Labor Service/AFSCME unit that also covers dispatchers, DPW, clerical, and administrative staff. No dedicated custodial wage table exists (custodians share the same unit-wide percentage-increase structure as everyone else in the unit), but real custodial-specific provisions exist: a dedicated Appendix B ("Custodial Vacancies") governing temporary/permanent custodial-position posting and bidding procedures; senior/junior building custodian tiers with a "compensation for higher rate" acting-up rule; shift-premium pay (40 cents/hour second shift, 60 cents/hour third shift); a specific, narrow outsourcing carve-out ("Cleaning of Heating Plants... shall be cleaned during the annual summer vacation by a company or contractor selected by the Town of Arlington"); and explicit inclusion of custodians in the DPW snow-incentive program.

**Short snippets:**
> Georgetown: "LICENSED MAINTENANCE ... STEP 1 ... 55,034.22" vs. "UNLICENSED MAINTENANCE ... STEP 1 ... 49,461.32"

> Franklin: "Junior Building Custodian $21.30 $22.33 $23.41 $24.54 $25.73" (5-step hourly schedule); "No subcontracting shall be used for normal cleaning of Public School buildings in order to avoid overtime, unless an emergency situation exists."

> Arlington: "Cleaning of Heating Plants - All boilers and furnaces in Town buildings shall be cleaned during the annual summer vacation by a company or contractor selected by the Town of Arlington."

**Classification: `confirmed_comparison_group`.**

**Recommended treatment:** Custodial/facilities should be carried forward as a real, evidenced non-safety comparison group. Two of the three rows (Georgetown, Franklin) have complete, quotable, multi-step salary schedules with genuine classification/credential tiers (Georgetown's licensed-vs-unlicensed maintenance split is a direct DPW-style credential-to-pay finding); the third (Arlington) adds real supplementary evidence even without its own wage table. The only remaining blocker is administrative, not evidentiary: `custodial`/`facilities` is still not a controlled-vocabulary value in `docs/schema.md`, so Georgetown's and Franklin's rows remain coded `other` pending that decision. All three rows' `binding_arbitration_statute` fields read MA G.L. c. 150E — ordinary, non-JLMC coverage, consistent with every other non-safety group.

## 5. Wayland nurse_health / AFSCME 1/2

**Evidence reviewed:** `ma_wayland_afscme_1_2_2020_2023.pdf` is an image-only, 48-page scanned document (Xerox AltaLink C8045 scanner output) with zero extractable native text (`pdftotext` returned 0 lines) — consistent with its `ocr_messy` quality flag. Per the task's allowance for a single bounded OCR attempt on a relevant image-only file, this session ran `pdftoppm` (200 DPI) followed by `tesseract` across all 48 pages (no corpus file was modified; the OCR output exists only as a temporary working file at `/tmp/evidence_review/`, not written back to `corpus/` or `data/contracts.csv`). The extraction succeeded cleanly (2,186 lines, legible running text) — a materially better result than the cover-page-only OCR this same file received in prior sessions.

**Findings:**
- **Nurse_health is substantively present, not merely incidental — the strongest finding of this entire review.** The recognition clause (Article 2) explicitly states: "By mutual agreement, effective July 1, 2005, the Town and the Union hereby recognize the positions of Community Health Nurses as members of this bargaining unit." Community Health Nurses have **their own pay grades** in Appendix A's wage schedule: **G-7A "Public Health Nurse"** ($75,946-$81,030 across steps 1-3) and **G-15 "Community Health Nurse"** ($56,848-$61,311 across steps 1-3), plus a related G-5 "Clinician" grade. A Masters-degree credential stipend exists: "Upon attaining a Masters' Degree Community Health Nurse and Public Health Nurse shall be eligible for an annual payment of $1,500. The degree must be in a relevant nursing or public health discipline" — a direct, DPW/library-style credential-to-pay linkage. A specific, dated administrative-oversight finding exists: "The Town and the Union agree that Community Health Nurses' wages are in need of adjustment as they were not included in the May 2022 Compensation Study due to their unique job structure (Town & Schools)" — a genuine, quotable instance of a non-safety wage-setting process being administratively deprioritized, directly on point for this project's non-safety-wage-restraint hypothesis (H17). A school-year-linked work schedule (182.5 days plus 4.5 additional/in-service days for a total of 187, "as determined by the Public Health Director") and a $65/hour summer-program rate are also visible.
- **Dispatchers are also substantively present in this same document.** The recognition clause separately covers "all regular full-time dispatch personnel" of the "Joint Communications Center." Article 19 (Pay Schedules) and Article 20 (Overtime) contain detailed dispatcher-specific provisions: shift differentials (6.0% evening, 7.0% night), a "civilian dispatcher" appointed by the Police Chief as Training Officer, State 911 Board certification requirements for part-time dispatch staff, EMD recertification language, and dedicated pay grades — **G-3 "JCC Dispatcher"** ($53,102-$56,631) and **G-4 "JCC Dispatcher Coordinator"** (grade table continues beyond the extracted range). This corroborates and extends Arlington's dispatcher findings with a second city's worth of evidence, including — unlike Arlington — an actual dollar-figure base-wage table for the dispatcher classification specifically.
- **Custodial/facilities is not present in this document.** No "custodian," "janitor," or "maintenance" keyword hit appears anywhere in the full 2,186-line OCR extraction. The recognition clause's actual membership (Community Health Nurses; DPW Water Treatment/Distribution Superintendent; DPW Highway/Transfer Station/Parks Superintendent; clerical staff in the Town Office, DPW, and Joint Communications Center) does not include custodial titles — the "and other positions" characterization used in this row's `total_comp_note` metadata field is more precisely described as a mix of nurses, dispatchers, senior DPW-superintendent titles, and a wide range of professional/administrative titles (Assessor, Treasurer/Collector, GIS Analyst, Conservation Administrator, Council on Aging Director, Youth Services Director, Sanitarian/Health Agent), not a custodial bundle.
- **The unit is far more fragmented than its name suggests.** This is not primarily a clerical/DPW unit with a few extra titles attached — it is an extremely heterogeneous, professional-and-technical municipal-employee unit spanning at least nine distinct professional fields (public health nursing, emergency dispatch, DPW senior management, assessing, treasury, recreation, conservation, GIS, youth services) under one collective bargaining agreement, a composition pattern more extreme than anything else this project has documented (exceeding even Arlington's already-fragmented Labor Service unit).

**Short snippets:**
> "By mutual agreement, effective July 1, 2005, the Town and the Union hereby recognize the positions of Community Health Nurses as members of this bargaining unit."

> "Upon attaining a Masters' Degree Community Health Nurse and Public Health Nurse shall be eligible for an annual payment of $1,500. The degree must be in a relevant nursing or public health discipline as determined by the Human Resources Director."

> "The Town and the Union agree that Community Health Nurses' wages are in need of adjustment as they were not included in the May 2022 Compensation Study due to their unique job structure (Town & Schools)."

> Wage table: "G-7A [Public Health Nurse] 75,946 78,447 81,030" / "G-15 [Community Health Nurse] 56,848 59,038 61,311."

**Classification: `confirmed_nurse_health_evidence`.**

**Recommended treatment:** Nurse_health should be upgraded from the prior session's "defer" recommendation — the evidence is substantive, quotable, wage/grade-visible, and includes a genuine credential-to-pay and wage-restraint (compensation-study-exclusion) finding, not merely an incidental keyword hit. It should not, however, be elevated to a full "strong comparison" group on par with teachers or DPW: it rests on a single row, in a single city, embedded in an unusually fragmented multi-professional unit that complicates any clean occupation-specific comparison. The recommended treatment is `keep_secondary` — a real, evidenced, but thin (one city, one row) comparison point requiring the same composition caution already applied to every other fragmented unit in this project's corpus (Arlington's Labor Service unit; Wayland's own DPW-superintendent titles). This document should not be re-OCR'd again; this session's extraction is complete and usable for future reference.
