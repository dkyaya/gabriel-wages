# Massachusetts non-safety factfinding records-request packet

Date drafted: 2026-06-23  
Status: draft / not submitted

## Purpose

This packet targets Massachusetts non-safety factfinding reports and contract-impasse interest-arbitration records needed to separate occupation effects from document-type effects in H1. The current GABRIEL pilot shows a safety/non-safety comparability-language gap, but the highest-scoring safety records are award-style documents while current non-safety comparators are mostly CBAs or MOAs.

The prior acquisition pass and the targeted reconnaissance memo found no final downloadable non-safety factfinding report or non-safety interest-arbitration award ready for ingestion. The next low-cost step is a narrow public-records/manual-acquisition packet aimed at DLR and a few city/school custodians.

## Custodian strategy

1. DLR first: request exact dockets/cases, final reports, disposition documents, indexes, and no-record confirmations.
2. City and school records access officers second: request party copies, case numbers, petitions, closing letters, certifications, reports, and settlement/withdrawal records for the highest-priority cities.
3. No broad scraping: do not scrape licensed/authenticated sources, do not bypass blockers, and do not use petitions or strike/representation decisions as final corpus documents.

## Intake checklist for returned files

- Save returned PDFs under `inbox/foia/` or another established inbox subfolder.
- Record custodian, retrieval date, retrieval method (`foia` unless direct public download), and exact source/cite.
- Perform first-page/entity check before any ingestion step.
- Confirm the Massachusetts city/employer from document text, not from the filename.
- Confirm `source_type` is truly `factfinding` or `arbitration_award`; otherwise classify correctly or do not ingest.
- Do not manually edit `contracts.csv`.
- Use `process_inbox.py` only after full documents are local and manifest rows are complete.
- Keep full documents in `corpus/` through the pipeline, not pasted into CSVs.
